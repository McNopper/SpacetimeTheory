# What is Time?

### *Time is geometric*

by *Norbert Nopper*

$$t = \frac{\sqrt{R^2 - r^2}}{c}$$

where

- $c$ is the speed of light
- $t$ is time
- $r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$ is the spatial distance
- $R$ is the radius of the hypersphere S³

## Premise

Every spacetime event is represented as a quaternion $q = ct + x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}$ and lies on a hypersphere S³ of radius $R$:

$$|q| = \sqrt{c^2t^2 + x^2 + y^2 + z^2} = R$$

## Constraint

On the hypersphere S³, every event must satisfy:

$$(ct)^2 + r^2 = R^2$$

The left side has two parts — a temporal part $(ct)^2$ and a spatial part $r^2$ — and together they must equal the total radius squared $R^2$. This means time and space are not independent: they share a finite budget set by $R$.

- An event at the origin ($r = 0$) has maximum time: $t = R/c$
- An event at maximum spatial distance ($r = R$) has no time: $t = 0$
- Everything in between is a trade-off

Since $R = \frac{2Gm}{c^2}$, where $G$ is the gravitational constant and $m$ is the total mass of the universe, this budget grows as mass forms:

$$(ct)^2 + r^2 = \left(\frac{2Gm}{c^2}\right)^2$$

More mass means a larger universe — and more room for both time and space.

## Solving for Time

From the constraint, solving for $t$:

$$c^2t^2 = R^2 - r^2$$

$$t = \frac{\sqrt{R^2 - r^2}}{c}$$

Substituting $R = \frac{2Gm}{c^2}$:

$$t = \frac{\sqrt{\dfrac{4G^2m^2}{c^4} - r^2}}{c}$$

Time is not an independent parameter — it is geometrically determined by your position on S³ and the total mass of the universe.
