# Quaternion-Hypersphere Theory of Spacetime

by *Norbert Nopper*

### Definition of a Quaternion

A quaternion is defined as:

$$q = a + b\mathbf{i} + c\mathbf{j} + d\mathbf{k}$$

where $a, b, c, d \in \mathbb{R}$ and the basis elements satisfy:

$$\mathbf{i}^2 = \mathbf{j}^2 = \mathbf{k}^2 = \mathbf{i}\mathbf{j}\mathbf{k} = -1$$

### Theory

A spacetime event is represented as a quaternion:

$$q = t + x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}$$

where the components map as $a \equiv t$, $b \equiv x$, $c \equiv y$, $d \equiv z$, with $t$ as time and $x, y, z$ as the spatial coordinates of the Cartesian coordinate system.

Any point $q$ lies on a **hypersphere S³** of radius $R$, satisfying:

$$|q| = \sqrt{t^2 + x^2 + y^2 + z^2} = R$$

This places time and space on equal footing with a Euclidean signature $(+,+,+,+)$.

All components $t, x, y, z$ are expressions of the same underlying energetic reality — spacetime **is** energy. The quaternion norm $|q| = R$ is directly proportional to the total energy of the universe:

$$E = \frac{c^4}{2G} R$$

### Expansion of the Universe

When energy converts to mass via $E = mc^2$, the radius $R$ of the hypersphere grows — the universe expands. Thus $R$ is not a constant but a function of the total mass formed:

$$R = \frac{2Gm}{c^2}$$

| State | Description | Hypersphere |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Singularity | Pure energy | $R = 0$ |
| Big Bang | Energy → mass begins | $R$ starts growing |
| Present | Ongoing conversion | $R$ is large |
| Future | Continued conversion | $R \to \infty$ |

Cosmic expansion is driven by the conversion $E = mc^2$, not by a cosmological constant.
